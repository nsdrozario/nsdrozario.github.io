Software

To get started with the music theory tutorials on this website, I recommend that you download a few programs on your computer. This will make music production much easier- the composition and recording process can be done much quicker on a computer. I recommend that you install a music notation program and a digital audio workstation. Some free examples I recommend are MuseScore and LMMS. (I am not affiliated with any of these projects or their owners/maintainers in any way. These links are provided for your convenience.)

Workflow

When creating compositions where music theory is used heavily, it is wise to first create a score, export it as a MIDI file, and import it into your digital audio workstation of choice. Music theory concepts are easier to apply to scores rather than a piano roll in a digital audio workstation because in a score, much more information is visible.

Further Notes

These tutorials will not post specific details on using these programs themselves, but rather general concepts that one can use for using these kinds of software in music production. This is so that articles may be concise with only relevant details only, which can be applied to a variety of environments. If you find yourself stuck on navigating your software of choice, I recommend using that software's documentation or other online tutorials dedicated specifically towards using that piece of software.

Exercises

  • Exercise 1: Create a score with a single treble clef staff with the following notes in ascending order: C, E, G.

  • Exercise 2: In your music notation software, export your score as a MIDI file.

  • Exercise 3: Import your MIDI file in your digital audio workstation.
